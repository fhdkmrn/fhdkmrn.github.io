Analyzing Messi and Ronaldo's "Tap-Ins"

Jan 5, 2018

Cristiano Ronaldo and Lionel Messi are undoubtebly two of the greatest football players in our generation, and arguably ever. Their specific strengths vary, with Ronaldo well known for his heading ability, shot power, athleticism, and skill moves, while Messi is revered for his dribbling abilities, playmaking, control, and composure. The fan bases of the two players are often at odds, with both sides attempting to prove why one of the players is better than the other. More often than not, both sides mention how both Ronaldo and Messi score many tap-in goals, seemingly easy goals where the player is open and unmarked only a few yards in-front of the goal. By analyzing how Ronaldo and Messi, often the most marked men on the pitch, get themselves in the position to score these sorts of goals, we can see how their off-ball movement is just another skill that the two have perfected and used to elevate themselves to being two of footballs greats.
